Burj Khalifa

Emirates

"The Burj Khalifa, formerly known as the Burj Dubai before its inauguration, stands as a monumental skyscraper in Dubai, United Arab Emirates, and holds the prestigious title of the world's tallest structure. Soaring to a total height of 829.8 meters (2,722 feet, or just over half a mile) and boasting a roof height of 828 meters (2,717 feet, excluding the antenna but including a 242.6-meter spire), the Burj Khalifa has reigned as the tallest building globally since its topping out in 2009. It surpassed the Taipei 101, the previous record-holder, and continues to be an iconic symbol of architectural achievement and innovation."

Merdeka 118

Malaysia

"Merdeka 118, formerly known as Menara Warisan Merdeka, KL 118, and PNB 118, is a towering 118-story megatall skyscraper gracing Kuala Lumpur's skyline. Standing proudly at 678.9 meters (2,227 feet), it secures the position of the second-tallest building and structure globally, surpassed only by the iconic Burj Khalifa at 828 meters (2,717 feet). Beyond its impressive height, Merdeka 118 serves as a symbol of Kuala Lumpur's commitment to architectural innovation and modernity on the international stage."

Shanghai Tower

China

"Shanghai Tower, a beacon of modern design, stands as the world's third-tallest building. Soaring over 630 meters with 128 floors, it graces Shanghai's skyline with its distinctive spiral form. This architectural marvel not only exemplifies China's commitment to innovation but also contributes to the city's dynamic and futuristic urban landscape."

Makkah Royal

Saudi Arabia

"The Makkah Clock Royal Tower, formerly known as Abraj Al Bait, stand as a government-owned complex of seven skyscraper hotels in Mecca, Saudi Arabia. Integral to the King Abdulaziz Endowment Project, these towers aim to modernize the city to better serve its pilgrims. Among them, the Makkah Clock Royal Tower, serving as the central hotel tower, not only ranks as the fourth-tallest building globally but also holds the sixth-tallest freestanding structure worldwide. Beyond its impressive stature, the clock tower houses the Clock Tower Museum across its top four floors, contributing to the cultural significance of this iconic landmark."

Burj Milad

Iran

"The Milad Tower, also known as the Tehran Tower, stands as a versatile landmark in Tehran, Iran. As the world's first telecommunication tower in terms of usage area at the top structure, it claims the title of the sixth-tallest tower globally. Not only is it the tallest tower in Iran, but it also ranks as the 24th-tallest freestanding structure worldwide. The meticulous construction of this iconic tower spanned approximately 11 years and 7 months, showcasing Iran's architectural prowess and technological advancement."
